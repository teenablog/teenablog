<h1>Pyridoxine Dicaprylate Market, Global Outlook and Forecast 2025-2032</h1><p><strong style="font-size:13px">Pyridoxine Dicaprylate</strong>, also known as <strong style="font-size:13px">Pyridoxine Dihexanoate</strong>, is a chemical compound that combines pyridoxine (a form of <strong style="font-size:13px">Vitamin B6</strong>) with <strong style="font-size:13px">caprylic acid</strong> (also called <strong style="font-size:13px">octanoic acid</strong>). It is an ester compound commonly used as a <strong style="font-size:13px">dietary supplement</strong> and a <strong style="font-size:13px">food additive</strong> due to its effectiveness in delivering Vitamin B6 to the body. Pyridoxine dicaprylate is known for its <strong style="font-size:13px">solubility in oils</strong> and <strong style="font-size:13px">bioavailability</strong>, which makes it easier for the body to absorb compared to regular pyridoxine.</p><p>
</p><p>Typically, this compound appears as a <strong>yellow powder</strong> and is utilized in various applications where <strong>Vitamin B6</strong> supplementation is required, particularly in conditions involving <strong>B6 deficiencies</strong>. Its primary uses include its incorporation into <strong>nutritional supplements</strong>, <strong>functional foods</strong>, and in <strong>pharmaceutical formulations</strong> aimed at improving <strong>Vitamin B6 intake</strong>.
