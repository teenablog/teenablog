</p><p>Wear Resistant Steel, often referred to as abrasion-resistant (AR) steel, is a type of carbon steel that has been specifically designed to withstand harsh wear environments. Its primary purpose is to offer superior resistance against abrasion, impact, and wear caused by friction. This steel is typically used in high-impact and wear-intensive industries such as mining, construction, agriculture, recycling, and heavy machinery manufacturing. The hardness of wear resistant steel is usually quantified through Brinell Hardness Number (HB), with variants like 400 HB, 450 HB, and 500 HB commonly available. The higher the HB rating, the greater the wear resistance.</p><p>
</p><p>Industries that deal with heavy machinery and require extended service life of components without frequent maintenance or replacements find wear resistant steel particularly valuable. It is extensively utilized in dump truck bodies, crushers, conveyor systems, cement mixers, and chutes—components that are constantly exposed to rugged operational environments.</p><p>
