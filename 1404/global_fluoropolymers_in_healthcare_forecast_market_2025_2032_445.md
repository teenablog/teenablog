</p><p>Fluoropolymers in healthcare refer to a class of high-performance polymers that are chemically inert, thermally stable, and extremely durable, making them particularly suited for medical and healthcare applications. These polymers, including polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF), fluorinated ethylene propylene (FEP), and perfluoroalkoxy alkane (PFA), are widely used in surgical instruments, catheters, tubing, and various medical devices. Their non-reactivity with body tissues and fluids, coupled with their ability to withstand sterilization processes, makes them indispensable in clinical and laboratory settings.</p><p>
</p><p>Fluoropolymers exhibit exceptional resistance to oils, solvents, acids, and other aggressive chemicals. Their low coefficient of friction, high dielectric strength, and resistance to ultraviolet radiation further extend their use across a wide range of healthcare applications, from diagnostics to therapeutics. As the healthcare sector continues to demand materials that ensure patient safety, cleanliness, and regulatory compliance, fluoropolymers are emerging as a niche yet vital material group within the medical field.</p><p>
