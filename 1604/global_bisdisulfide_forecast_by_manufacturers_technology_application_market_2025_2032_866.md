</p><p>Bis-(sodium sulfopropyl)-disulfide, often abbreviated as SPS or BSPD, is an organosulfur compound primarily used in the electroplating industry. It serves as a brightener and leveling agent, particularly in copper electroplating processes. Its molecular structure, featuring disulfide linkages and sulfonate groups, makes it highly soluble in water and stable under a wide range of electrochemical conditions. This compound is commonly used in printed circuit board (PCB) manufacturing, semiconductor applications, and other electronic component production.</p><p>
</p><p>Due to its effectiveness in improving deposit properties, reducing internal stress, and enhancing the uniformity of metal coatings, SPS is increasingly adopted as a standard additive in modern plating formulations. It also exhibits favorable environmental and safety profiles compared to traditional plating additives, positioning it as a sustainable alternative in industrial surface treatment operations.</p><p>
