These plates are typically alloyed with chromium, nickel, molybdenum, and other elements that enhance their thermal stability and resistance to wear and deformation. Such characteristics make them indispensable in heavy industries such as mining, metallurgy, power generation, petrochemical, cement, and construction.</p><p>

These properties enable High Temperature Wear-resistant Steel Plates to deliver prolonged service life and reduced maintenance in high-stress, high-temperature settings, where standard steel would fail prematurely. Common applications include kiln components, furnace liners, incinerators, and parts of high-temperature conveyors.</p><p>
