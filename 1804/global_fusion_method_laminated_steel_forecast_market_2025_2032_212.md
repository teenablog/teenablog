Market Size</p><p>
</p><p><strong>Fusion Method Laminated Steel</strong>, also known as the "Fukuyama Method," refers to a specialized manufacturing process for creating laminated steel sheets. This method involves bonding multiple layers of different materials, typically steel and non-ferrous metals like aluminum or copper, through a high-temperature rolling process. The resulting composite material offers the strength and durability of steel combined with the lightweight and corrosion-resistant properties of non-ferrous metals. This fusion technique is integral to industries that require high-performance materials, such as automotive, construction, electronics, and packaging.</p><p>
