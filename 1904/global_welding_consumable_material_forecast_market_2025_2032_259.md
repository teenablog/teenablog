</p><p><strong>Welding consumable materials refer to the substances consumed during the welding process. These include electrodes, filler metals, flux, shielding gases, and other materials that facilitate the joining of metals. Among them, flux plays a critical role in forming a protective gas shield around the weld pool, preventing oxidation and impurities. It acts as a deoxidizer, eliminating oxygen from the molten metal and preventing porosity formation. Meanwhile, filler metals are melted and added to the base metal to create a strong, cohesive weld joint. Collectively, flux and filler metals are referred to as welding consumables, essential for processes like arc welding, MIG, TIG, and submerged arc welding.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>The demand for welding consumable materials is directly tied to industrial and infrastructure development, including construction, automotive manufacturing, shipbuilding, oil and gas, and energy. Their role in ensuring welding integrity and structural durability underscores their irreplaceable importance in manufacturing and heavy industries.</strong></p><p>
