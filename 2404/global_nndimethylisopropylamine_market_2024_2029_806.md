</p><p>N,N-Dimethylisopropylamine (DMIPA) is a colorless liquid with the chemical formula C5H13N. It is an acyclic tertiary amine and has a boiling point of 68°C, a density of 0.72 g/cm³ at 20°C, and a flash point of -27°C. DMIPA is used in various applications, including as a protonating agent for nitrogen-containing compounds in the gas phase. It is also used in the production of surfactants, such as cocamidopropyl betaine, which are used in personal care products like soaps, shampoos, and cosmetics.
