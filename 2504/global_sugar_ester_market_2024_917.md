</p><p>Sugar esters are a type of non-ionic biological surfactant obtained through the esterification of glucose and sucrose with fatty acids. They are widely used in food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and industrial applications due to their emulsifying, stabilizing, and foaming properties. Sugar esters are categorized into monoesters, diesters, and triesters based on the degree of esterification. Their non-toxic and biodegradable nature makes them an eco-friendly alternative to traditional surfactants.</p><p>
