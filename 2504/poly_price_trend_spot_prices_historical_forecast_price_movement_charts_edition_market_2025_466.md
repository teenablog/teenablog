Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) is a highly versatile material, and its demand is driven by several key applications across various industries. One of the primary drivers is the construction sector, where PVC is extensively used in piping, window profiles, flooring, and wall coverings due to its durability, water resistance, and cost-effectiveness. The demand for infrastructure development, urbanization, and housing projects continues to fuel growth in this sector. PVC is also widely used in the automotive industry for parts like dashboards, door panels, and wiring insulation, benefiting from its lightweight and durable nature. The packaging industry is another major consumer, where PVC is used for products such as bottles, cling films, and blister packs, offering both strength and clarity. Additionally, PVCs use in medical devices, including blood bags, tubing, and intravenous bags, continues to expand, particularly with advancements in healthcare and the rising demand for medical equipment. The electrical industry also relies on PVC for insulation in wires and cables due to its high dielectric properties and resistance to environmental factors.
