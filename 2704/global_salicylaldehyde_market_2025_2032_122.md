This colorless oily liquid has a bitter almond odor at higher concentrations, and is widely recognized for its aromatic properties. It is commonly used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, fragrances, and the synthesis of specialty chemicals. Salicylaldehyde is the second isomer in the hydroxybenzaldehyde group, alongside 3-hydroxybenzaldehyde and 4-hydroxybenzaldehyde, differing in the position of the hydroxyl group on the benzene ring. The molecule has significant applications due to its reactivity in organic synthesis, including its role as a precursor to drugs, cosmetics, and even herbicides.</p><p>
