</p><p><strong>White Graphite (Hexagonal Boron Nitride)</strong> is a unique material often referred to as "white graphite" due to its striking resemblance to graphite in structure and properties. It consists of boron and nitrogen atoms arranged in a hexagonal crystal lattice, similar to the atomic arrangement found in graphite. This structure endows hexagonal BN with a range of beneficial properties, including excellent thermal conductivity, electrical insulation, and lubrication capabilities, particularly under high-temperature conditions.</p><p>
</p><p>Hexagonal Boron Nitride is primarily used as a dry lubricant, as it reduces friction and wear, making it a critical component in industries such as metallurgy, aerospace, electronics, and cosmetics. Additionally, it serves as a vital ingredient in the creation of boron nitride-based composite ceramics, cubic boron nitride (CBN), and refractory materials. It is also valued in the electronics industry for its thermal and electrical insulating properties, which contribute to the safe and efficient operation of sensitive electronic components.</p><p>
