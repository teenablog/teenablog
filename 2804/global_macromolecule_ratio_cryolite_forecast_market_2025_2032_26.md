</p><p>Macromolecule Ratio Cryolite, also known as high-molecular-ratio cryolite, refers to a specialized type of synthetic cryolite in which the sodium molecular ratio lies between 2.8 and 3.0. Unlike standard cryolite, this polymer-based variant contains fewer impurities and offers a higher actual yield, making it a more efficient choice in industrial applications.</p><p>
</p><p>Primarily used in the aluminum smelting process, macromolecule ratio cryolite acts as a flux to lower the melting point of aluminum oxide, thereby optimizing energy usage. Its refined purity and high molecular ratio have positioned it as a preferred option among cryolite customers seeking efficiency and improved performance.</p><p>
