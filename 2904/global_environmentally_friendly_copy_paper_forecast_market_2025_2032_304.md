</p><p><strong>Environmentally friendly copy paper refers to paper products specifically manufactured to minimize environmental impact throughout their lifecycle. These papers are produced using sustainable raw materials, such as recycled fibers or pulp sourced from responsibly managed forests certified by organizations like the Forest Stewardship Council (FSC) or the Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification (PEFC). Additionally, the manufacturing processes aim to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, energy and water consumption, and chemical usage.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Key characteristics of environmentally friendly copy paper include:</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>Recycled content: Often made from post-consumer or pre-consumer recycled paper.</strong></p><p><strong>Chlorine-free processing: Avoids harmful bleaching chemicals like chlorine gas.</strong></p><p><strong>Sustainable sourcing: Derived from sustainably managed forests.</strong></p><p><strong>Biodegradability: Easily decomposes without leaving harmful residues.</strong></p><p>
