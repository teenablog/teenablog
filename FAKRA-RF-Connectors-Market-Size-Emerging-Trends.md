FAKRA connectors are utilized within the automotive industry. Standard uses for FAKRA connectors are coaxial connections on devices with external antennas; such applications include SDARS, Cellular, GPS Navigation, key-less entry and satellite radio.

FAKRA RF connectors are a type of high-frequency coaxial connector used to transmit signals in automotive applications. They are designed to provide a secure and reliable connection between components in a vehicleâ€™s communication and entertainment systems, including GPS, satellite radio, Bluetooth, and Wi-Fi. The FAKRA connector system was developed by the German Association of the Automotive Industry (VDA) and is widely used in European and American automobiles. These connectors are designed to be water-resistant and feature a color-coded coding system to ensure proper mating and prevent mismating during installation.

Recent Developments:
In July 2021, TE Connectivity introduced a new line of FAKRA mini coaxial connectors designed for automotive applications, offering reduced connector heights and simplified mating interfaces.
In February 2021, Amphenol RF expanded its line of FAKRA connectors to include options with white insulators, which are used in some European car models.
In September 2020, Molex announced the launch of its FAKRA II SMB connector system, which offers a higher frequency range and smaller form factor than the original FAKRA system.
In July 2020, Rosenberger introduced a new FAKRA push-pull connector system for automotive applications, offering a space-saving design and easy installation process.
In January 2020, Radiall released a new range of FAKRA connectors designed for high-frequency applications, offering a broad range of impedance options and a low profile design.
