Handheld imagers refer to portable devices equipped with imaging technology that allows users to capture and analyze visual information. These devices are designed to be held and operated by hand, making them convenient for a wide range of applications. Handheld imagers typically include cameras, sensors, and image processing capabilities, and they are used in various industries and scenarios for tasks such as capturing images, reading barcodes, conducting inspections, and more. Some common examples of handheld imagers include barcode scanners, thermal imaging cameras, digital cameras, and portable medical imaging devices. They are valuable tools for data collection, quality control, diagnostics, and various other visual-based tasks.

Market Trends: 
The increasing demand for security: The increasing concerns about security and terrorism are driving the demand for handheld imagers in a variety of applications, such as law enforcement, border security, and private security.
The growing adoption of handheld imagers in the industrial sector: Handheld imagers are increasingly being used in the industrial sector for applications such as non-destructive testing, machine vision, and quality control.
The development of new and innovative handheld imager technologies: New handheld imager technologies are being developed that are more sensitive, accurate, and affordable. This is making handheld imagers more accessible to a wider range of users.
Military and Defense:
Handheld thermal imagers are extensively used by the military, army, and navy for border surveillance and law enforcement activities.
They are deployed in ship collision avoidance and guidance systems, improving navigation and safety at sea.
In military aviation, thermal imaging helps mitigate risks during low light and night operations, enhancing situational awareness and safety.
Public Safety and Law Enforcement:
Handheld thermal imagers provide tactical advantages to firefighters, law enforcement officers, and search and rescue teams.
They are essential for operations in total darkness, enabling the clear detection of individuals, victims, or suspects.
These systems offer mission-critical information that can save lives and aid in locating individuals in challenging conditions.
Technological Advancements:
The military often serves as a testing ground for technological innovations, with a focus on improving security and surveillance.
Military activities seek solutions, including multi-sensor surveillance systems and thermal imaging cameras, to enhance situational awareness and security.
The use of thermal imaging contributes to route clearance and reconnaissance missions, particularly in areas susceptible to improvised explosive devices (IEDs) and ambushes.
