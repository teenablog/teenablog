key trends related to the MEMS based oscillator market:

Miniaturization – There is an increasing demand for smaller and lower power oscillators. MEMS technology allows oscillators to be made much smaller than traditional quartz crystals. This drives adoption in space/weight constrained applications like smartphones.
Improved performance – MEMS oscillators can offer better frequency stability over temperature variations compared to traditional oscillators. This makes them suitable for demanding applications.
Lower cost – By leveraging semiconductor batch fabrication techniques, MEMS oscillators can be mass produced at a lower cost. This enables their adoption in cost sensitive mainstream markets.
Timing applications – MEMS oscillators are seeing high adoption as timing references in communication, automotive, and industrial applications requiring small form factors and stable frequencies.
Consumer devices – The smartphone and wearables markets are driving high volume demand for MEMS timing components like oscillators and resonators. Market research expects rapid growth in this segment.
Diversification – While initial MEMS oscillator designs targeted replacing quartz, new and optimized designs are emerging for specialized applications like real-time clocks, jitter attenuation, spread spectrum output etc.
Major opportunities in the MEMS based oscillator market:

Mobile Devices – The boom in smartphones and wearables presents a massive volume opportunity for MEMS oscillators. As devices get smaller, MEMS can replace bulky quartz crystals. The market for timing devices in mobile is expected to reach billions of units.
Automotive Applications – Modern vehicles require a number of precise, stable clock signals for systems like GPS, WiFi, Bluetooth etc. MEMS oscillators are more robust to vibration/shock in automotive environments. Vehicle production volumes will drive growth.
Communications Infrastructure – 5G, wireline telecom infrastructure requires high performance, temperature resistant oscillators at the circuit board level. MEMS is suitable for front-end modules, control plane timing etc.
Aerospace/Defense – Rigorous environment performance and miniaturization demands make aerospace/defense attractive for MEMS oscillator suppliers. Applications include guidance systems, radios, mission computers etc.
Wearables and IoT Devices – The need for small, energy efficient timing devices in smartwatches, fitness trackers, VR headsets etc. provides growth opportunities as the IoT market expands.
Emerging Markets – Countries like China and India with large smartphone and consumer electronics manufacturers offer viable high volume opportunities. Local manufacturing is incentives for suppliers.
