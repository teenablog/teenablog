Market Overview

Recycled DTY (Draw Textured Yarn) refers to a type of yarn made from recycled polyester materials, which undergoes a process known as draw texturing. The draw texturing process involves drawing and twisting the fiber to give it a textured, elastic nature, which enhances its bulkiness and stretchability. This type of yarn is widely used in the textile industry due to its ability to mimic the properties of virgin yarn while being more eco-friendly.

DTY yarn is primarily used in applications requiring stretch and durability, such as in apparel, home textiles, and automotive fabrics. Recycled DTY yarn takes it a step further by using post-consumer or post-industrial polyester waste, including plastic bottles and used garments, which are reprocessed to create high-quality yarn. This not only reduces waste but also lowers the environmental impact associated with manufacturing virgin polyester.

Recycled DTY yarn plays a crucial role in promoting sustainability in the textile industry. By utilizing recycled materials, manufacturers can reduce energy consumption, minimize carbon emissions, and lessen their dependence on petroleum-based raw materials.
