Synthetic graphite is a man-made material produced by heating carbon-rich materials, such as petroleum coke and coal tar pitch, at high temperatures. Synthetic graphite materials have various applications across different industries due to their unique properties, including high electrical and thermal conductivity, resistance to corrosion and heat, and lubricity.

key types of synthetic graphite materials:



Synthetic graphite electrodes: Used in electric arc furnaces for steel production and in the smelting of other metals.
Synthetic graphite anodes: Used in the production of lithium-ion batteries, which power electric vehicles and portable electronic devices.
Specialty synthetic graphite materials: Used in various applications, including high-temperature seals, bearings, heat exchangers, and crucibles.
Nuclear graphite: Used in nuclear reactors as a moderator and reflector of neutrons.
Carbon fiber reinforced carbon (CFRC): A composite material made from carbon fibers embedded in a carbon matrix, used in aerospace, defense, and other high-performance applications.
The demand for synthetic graphite materials is expected to grow due to the increasing use of electric vehicles and renewable energy, as well as the development of advanced materials for various industrial applications.
