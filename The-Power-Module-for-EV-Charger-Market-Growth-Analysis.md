Major trends shaping developments in the power module for electric vehicle charger market include:



Higher power density modules: There is a focus on innovating with advanced materials like SiC and GaN along with superior thermal designs to enable more compact form factors capable of delivering over 200kW power.

Bi-directional charging capabilities: Power modules supporting vehicle-to-grid (V2G) bi-directional charging are an emerging trend to facilitate grid energy storage and demand response solutions using EV batteries.

Support for ultra-fast charging: 800V+ power modules optimized to handle ultra-fast charging up to 350 kW capacity are coming into demand to reduce charging times.

Multi-protocol chargers: Power modules enabling flexible Multi-protocol or "universal" chargers compliant with combined AC, DC charging standards to suit different vehicle types and grids are growing in scope.

AI and diagnostics integration: Intelligent power modules with embedded sensors, diagnostics and analytics for condition monitoring, predictive maintenance and automated safety are an advancing domain.

Standardization of form factors: Defining unified power module designs for easier assembly and cooling interoperability is gathering focus for economies of scale across EV platforms.
